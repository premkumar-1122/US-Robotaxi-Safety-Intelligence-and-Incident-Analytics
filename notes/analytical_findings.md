# Analytical Findings & Operational Insights
## US Robotaxi Safety Intelligence & Incident Analytics

### 1. Overall Safety & Severity Profile
Robotaxi operational incidents are overwhelmingly low-severity, interaction-driven events.
* **No Injury:** ~88% of all recorded incidents resulted in property damage only.
* **Minor Injury:** ~10% of incidents.
* **Severe Outcomes:** The dataset contains only 1 fatality and extremely rare moderate injuries.
* **Insight:** Most robotaxi collisions resemble low-speed urban friction (parking contacts, intersection negotiation) rather than catastrophic autonomous failures.

### 2. Operational Environment Context
Deployments are fundamentally constrained to dense urban landscapes, which drives the incident profile.
* **Roadway Type:** Intersections present the highest proportion of injuries (~11.8% minor injury), aligning with the complex nature of cross-traffic, signal negotiation, and occlusions. Parking lots, while frequent sites for incidents, have a 97% No-Injury rate.
* **ODD Compliance:** 98.9% of all incidents occurred *inside* the vehicles' Operational Design Domain (ODD). This proves that edge cases are generated by real-world urban complexity, not vehicles wandering outside their mapped territories.
* **Weather:** Clear weather completely dominates the dataset (680 incidents vs. 38 in rain). Rain does not show elevated injury severity. **Finding:** Environmental weather is not the primary risk driver; traffic interaction is.

### 3. Geographic Concentration
US Robotaxi deployments are highly regionalized into a few mature, AV-friendly ecosystems.
* **State Dominance:** California (54.7%), Texas (18.5%), and Arizona (16.1%) account for ~89% of all incidents.
* **City Epicenters:** San Francisco, Los Angeles, Austin, and Phoenix are the core operational hubs, heavily skewing risk toward high-complexity urban metrics.

### 4. Interaction Complexity (Crash Counterparts)
* **Passenger Vehicles:** Comprise the vast majority of collisions (68.6%).
* **Commercial Vehicles:** Make up 13.8% of collisions but show primarily low-severity outcomes, likely due to predictable routing and professional drivers.
* **Vulnerable Road Users (VRUs) & Two-Wheelers:** While extremely rare (~2.5% combined), these incidents carry a disproportionate risk. **58% of VRU incidents result in minor injuries and 8% in moderate injuries.** This is the highest severity cluster in the dataset.

### 5. Deployment Scaling vs. Safety Degradation
* **Temporal Scaling:** Incident volume jumped from ~60/month in mid-2025 to ~118/month in late 2025/early 2026.
* **Q4 2025 Inflection:** Waymo exhibited a massive scaling inflection starting in October 2025 (jumping from ~56 to ~86 incidents/month).
* **Insight:** This curve mirrors commercial fleet scaling, geographic expansion, and broader operational exposure, rather than a degradation of the core autonomous driving software.

### 6. Reporting Maturity Pipeline
* **Submission Speed:** 97% of incidents are reported within 0–1 months, demonstrating that operator monitoring systems successfully identify and escalate incidents rapidly.
* **Severity Correlation:** Serious incidents (fatalities, moderate injuries) are predominantly reported within 0 months. Long-tail reporting delays (4-8 months) are almost exclusively associated with No-Injury or Minor-Injury cases, proving that delayed reporting is an administrative/amendment artifact, not an attempt to hide severe crashes.
